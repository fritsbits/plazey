## Design Context

### Users
Brussels neighbourhood residents — families, children, language learners (NL + FR), volunteers, and people who want to contribute a project. Casual, community context: users arrive via Facebook shares, word of mouth, or physical flyers. Not a tech-savvy audience. Many access on mobile. Readability must work for tired people, kids, and people reading in a second language.

### Brand Personality
Warm, bubbly, accessible — the energy of a neighbourhood party, not a polished festival brand. Honest and direct. Nothing that feels corporate, distant, or over-designed.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Now (wireframe phase):** Grayscale only. Caprasimo for headings, system-ui for body. Huisstijl tokens will replace CSS custom properties in `global.css` — no markup changes needed.

**Huisstijl target:** Bold & festive — strong colours, expressive typography, poster-like composition. Feels like something the community could have made, not a top-down brand rollout.

**Explicit anti-references:** Generic festival look — dark backgrounds, neon accents, countdown timers, DJ poster aesthetics. Nothing that looks like a ticketed concert site.

### Design Principles
1. **Type carries character.** Caprasimo as the display face is the main personality signal. Body copy supports it — legible, calm, never competing.
2. **Bold, not loud.** Festive energy comes from confident colour and composition, not visual noise or decoration for its own sake.
3. **Readable by everyone.** Short sentences, clear hierarchy, WCAG AA minimum. Designed for language learners and small screens first.
4. **Community-first aesthetics.** A little roughness or warmth beats slick polish. Poster quality over corporate finish.
5. **Huisstijl-ready at all times.** Every design decision must survive a token swap. No hard-coded values, no assumptions about final brand colours.
